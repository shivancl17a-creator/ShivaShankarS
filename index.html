About Me

Protein Biochemist & Structural Biologist

I am a protein biochemist and structural biologist with deep expertise in protein engineering, protein design, and mass spectrometry-based proteomics. Over 7+ years of doctoral and pre-doctoral research, I have developed a strong command of both experimental and computational approaches — from recombinant expression and biophysical characterization to cross-linking mass spectrometry, DIA/SWATH proteomics, and computational structure prediction using AlphaFold2 and inverse folding tools.

A defining theme of my research has been tackling difficult proteins: using structural insight and computational design to transform insoluble targets into functional, soluble variants, and building targeted proteomics workflows to interrogate post-translational modifications at the molecular level. This work culminated in co-inventing a patented glycation-resistant insulin therapeutic (WO 2024/194897 A1), combining protein engineering with detailed mass spectrometric characterization.

My structural biology foundation — built through crystallography training at CSIR-NCL and IISER-Pune and deepened through computational structure prediction — informs how I approach protein engineering problems: structure first, then design. I am passionate about merging this structural perspective with modern proteomics to understand how proteins are modified, how they interact, and how they can be redesigned for biomedical applications.

Research Narrative

Experience & Projects

Senior Research Fellow / Ph.D. (2017–2025)

CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory, Pune

Meteorin and Meteorin-like Protein Characterization

Conducted comprehensive structural and functional analysis of Meteorin family proteins in cellular metabolism. Utilized AlphaFold2/ColabFold for de novo structure prediction to identify functional receptor-binding regions and disease-associated missense variants. Developed a targeted proteomics assay (DIA/SWATH and PRM) for Metrnl quantification in biological samples. Integrated cross-linking mass spectrometry (XL-MS) with computational modeling to elucidate protein–protein interaction networks, and applied inverse folding (ProteinMPNN) to successfully design soluble variants of mouse Meteorin-like protein.

  • First structural characterization of Meteorin family proteins via computational prediction
  • Developed targeted proteomics workflows for Metrnl detection and quantification
  • Applied inverse folding design to rescue solubility and restore functional activity
Glycation-Resistant Insulin Development

Engineered a novel glycation-resistant insulin proteoform by combining de novo solubility-tag design with inverse folding strategies. Developed an original in vitro glycation workflow and characterized modification profiles by both bottom-up and top-down mass spectrometry. This translational protein engineering effort addressed a clinically meaningful challenge in diabetes therapy and resulted in a co-invented patent (WO 2024/194897 A1).

  • Designed novel in vitro workflow for studying insulin glycation mechanisms
  • Characterized glycation-modified insulin glargine by top-down mass spectrometry
  • Optimized expression and refolding systems, achieving significant yield improvements
SARS-CoV-2 Main Protease Inhibitor Discovery

Contributed to the computational discovery of multi-target-directed ligands against host-specific SARS-CoV-2 main protease through structural analysis and molecular docking, contributing to an early-pandemic publication.

Project Assistant II (2013–2017)

CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory, Pune

Santalene Synthase Structural Characterization

Developed end-to-end structural biology workflow for Santalene synthase from Indian sandalwood (Santalum album): cloning, site-directed mutagenesis, recombinant expression, multi-step chromatographic purification, and biophysical characterization. Conducted extensive crystallization screening using hanging-drop and sitting-drop vapor diffusion methods, including robotic crystallization platforms (Hamilton, Mosquito), and X-ray diffraction analysis. Contributed to a publication in Scientific Reports.

Junior Research Fellow (2010–2013)

IISER-Pune

HIV-1 Fusion Inhibitors and Antimicrobial Lipopeptides

Synthesized and structurally characterized gamma and hybrid gamma peptides designed as HIV-1 fusion inhibitors. Investigated the self-assembly behavior and broad-spectrum antimicrobial properties of short hybrid E-vinylogous lipopeptides, resulting in a first-author publication in the Journal of Medicinal Chemistry.

Project Trainee (2009–2010)

National Institute of Immunology (NII), New Delhi

Mycobacterium tuberculosis Histidine Biosynthesis Proteins

Cloned, expressed, and purified HisA, HisB, and HisC2 proteins involved in the histidine biosynthesis pathway of Mycobacterium tuberculosis. Conducted preliminary crystallization screening to obtain diffraction-quality crystals for structural characterization.

Research Philosophy & Future Directions

My scientific philosophy centers on the idea that deep structural understanding of a protein is the prerequisite for engineering it. Across every stage of my career — from crystallization screening at NII and structural characterization at CSIR-NCL, to de novo computational design and mass spectrometric dissection of protein modifications — I have worked to integrate structure, function, and design into a unified experimental framework.

Looking ahead, I am eager to apply this integrated approach at the frontier of protein design and protein engineering, particularly in contexts where structural biology and mass spectrometry proteomics work in concert: mapping interaction interfaces with XL-MS to guide computational redesign, using DIA/SWATH workflows to interrogate modified proteoforms, and leveraging inverse folding to rationally engineer proteins with improved biophysical properties and therapeutic potential.
