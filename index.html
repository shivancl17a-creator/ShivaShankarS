Research Narrative

Ph.D. Research (2017-2024)

CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory, Pune

Meteorin and Meteorin-like Protein Characterization

Conducted comprehensive structural and functional analysis of Meteorin family proteins in cellular metabolism. Utilized AlphaFold2 for de novo structure prediction to identify functional receptor-binding regions and disease-associated missense variants. Integrated cross-linking mass spectrometry (XL-MS) with computational modeling to elucidate protein-protein interaction networks.

  • First to solve structural features using computational prediction
  • Developed targeted proteomics assays for Metrnl detection
  • Applied inverse folding to design soluble functional variants
Glycation-Resistant Insulin Development

Engineered a novel glycation-resistant insulin proteoform by integrating de novo solubility-tag design with inverse folding approaches (ProteinMPNN). This work addresses a critical challenge in diabetes therapy - insulin degradation through non-enzymatic glycation. Successfully transformed an insoluble protein into a functional therapeutic candidate, resulting in a patent (WO 2024/194897 A1).

  • Developed novel invitro workflow for studying insulin glycation mechanisms
  • Optimized expression systems achieving significant yield improvements
  • Characterized modifications using top-down mass spectrometry
SARS-CoV-2 Main Protease Inhibitor Discovery

Contributed to the discovery of multi-target-directed ligands against SARS-CoV-2 main protease through computational screening and structural analysis, published during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Project Assistant II (2013-2017)

CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory, Pune

Santalene Synthase Structural Characterization

Executed cloning, mutations, expression, purification, crystallization, and biophysical characterization of Santalene synthase from Indian sandalwood. Gained extensive experience in protein crystallography and structural biology techniques.

Junior Research Fellow (2010-2013)

IISER-Pune

HIV-1 Fusion Inhibitor and Antimicrobial Lipopeptides Development

Synthesized and characterized gamma and hybrid gamma peptides as HIV-1 fusion inhibitors. Investigated antimicrobial activity of self-assembling short hybrid gamma lipopeptides, resulting in publication in Journal of Medicinal Chemistry.

Research Philosophy & Future Directions

My research trajectory demonstrates a commitment to solving fundamental biological questions through technological innovation. I excel at integrating experimental and computational approaches - from bench work (protein expression, purification, proteomics) to advanced computational modeling (AlphaFold2, molecular dynamics, inverse folding).

My core interest lies at the intersection of structural proteomics, protein engineering, and therapeutic development. I am particularly passionate about leveraging Protein design, proteomics, cross-linking mass spectrometry (XL-MS) and computational structure prediction to understand protein dynamics and engineer proteins with enhanced properties for biomedical applications.

I seek postdoctoral opportunities where I can apply my unique skill set in proteomics method development and protein engineering, with particular interest in understanding protein evolution, protein complexes, signaling networks, and developing targeted therapeutics.
