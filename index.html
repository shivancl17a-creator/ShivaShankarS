About Me

About Me

Protein Biochemist

I am a protein biochemist and structural biologist with expertise in protein engineering, protein design, and mass spectrometry–based proteomics. My work brings together hands-on experimental biochemistry and computational approaches, including structure prediction and inverse folding methods, to understand how protein structure governs function and how it can be rationally redesigned.

A recurring theme in my research has been working with challenging protein systems. I use structure-guided strategies to convert insoluble or poorly behaved proteins into functional variants and develop targeted proteomics workflows to probe protein modifications and interactions. Through this integrated approach, I aim to engineer proteins with improved stability, functionality, and biomedical relevance.

Experience & Projects

Senior Research Fellow / Ph.D. (2017–2025)

CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory, Pune

Meteorin and Meteorin-like Protein Characterization

Conducted comprehensive structural and functional analysis of Meteorin family proteins in cellular metabolism. Utilized AlphaFold2/ColabFold for de novo structure prediction to identify functional receptor-binding regions and disease-associated missense variants. Developed a targeted proteomics assay (PRM) for Metrnl quantification in biological samples. Integrated cross-linking mass spectrometry (XL-MS) with computational modeling to elucidate protein–protein interaction networks, and applied inverse folding (ProteinMPNN) to successfully design soluble variants of mouse Meteorin-like protein.

  • First structural characterization of Meteorin family proteins via computational prediction
  • Developed targeted proteomics workflows for Metrnl detection and quantification
  • Applied inverse folding design to rescue solubility and restore functional activity
Glycation-Resistant Insulin Development

Engineered a novel glycation-resistant insulin proteoform by combining de novo solubility-tag design for purification strategies. Developed an original in vitro glycation workflow and characterized modification profiles by both bottom-up and top-down mass spectrometry. This translational protein engineering effort addressed a clinically meaningful challenge in diabetes therapy and resulted in a co-invented patent (WO 2024/194897 A1).

  • Designed novel in vitro workflow for studying insulin glycation mechanisms
  • Characterized glycation-modified insulin glargine by top-down mass spectrometry
  • Optimized expression and refolding systems, achieving significant yield improvements
SARS-CoV-2 Main Protease Inhibitor Discovery

Contributed to the computational discovery of multi-target-directed ligands against host-specific SARS-CoV-2 main protease through structural analysis and molecular docking, contributing to an early-pandemic publication.

Project Assistant II (2013–2017)

CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory, Pune

Santalene Synthase Structural Characterization

Developed end-to-end protein purification and crystallization workflow for Santalene synthase from Indian sandalwood (Santalum album): cloning, site-directed mutagenesis, recombinant expression, multi-step chromatographic purification, and biophysical characterization. Conducted extensive crystallization screening using hanging-drop and sitting-drop vapor diffusion methods, including robotic crystallization platforms, and X-ray diffraction analysis.

Junior Research Fellow (2010–2013)

IISER-Pune

HIV-1 Fusion Inhibitors and Antimicrobial Lipopeptides

Synthesized and structurally characterized gamma and hybrid gamma peptides designed as HIV-1 fusion inhibitors.

Investigated the self-assembly behavior and broad-spectrum antimicrobial properties of short hybrid E-vinylogous lipopeptides, resulting in a first-author publication in the Journal of Medicinal Chemistry.

Project Trainee (2009–2010)

National Institute of Immunology (NII), New Delhi

Mycobacterium tuberculosis Histidine Biosynthesis Proteins

Cloned, expressed, and purified HisA, HisB, and HisC2 proteins involved in the histidine biosynthesis pathway of Mycobacterium tuberculosis. Conducted preliminary crystallization screening to obtain diffraction-quality crystals for structural characterization.

Research Philosophy & Future Directions

My scientific philosophy is rooted in the idea that effective protein engineering begins with a deep understanding of structure. Throughout my training, I have worked across experimental and computational boundaries ranging from crystallization screening and structural analysis to mass spectrometry–based characterization of protein states to build mechanistic insight before attempting design or modification.

Looking ahead, I am eager to pursue postdoctoral research at the intersection of protein design and protein engineering, particularly in contexts where protein design, structural biology and mass spectrometry proteomics work in concert: mapping protein interaction interfaces and workflows to interrogate modified proteoforms, and leveraging inverse folding to rationally engineer proteins with improved biophysical properties and therapeutic potential.
